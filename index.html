Our Hatchery Process

1

Breeding Stock Selection

We carefully select healthy, mature fish with desirable traits to serve as our breeding stock. Our selection process ensures genetic diversity and disease resistance in our fingerlings.

2

Spawning & Egg Collection

Under controlled conditions, we induce spawning and collect the fertilized eggs. Our temperature-controlled tanks provide the perfect environment for this critical stage.

3

Egg Incubation

The collected eggs are placed in specialized incubation systems where water quality, temperature, and oxygen levels are meticulously monitored until hatching occurs.

4

Larval Rearing

After hatching, the larvae are transferred to rearing tanks where they receive specialized feed and care during their most vulnerable developmental stage.

5

Fingerling Production

As the fish grow into fingerlings, we gradually acclimate them to conditions similar to those they'll encounter in ponds or natural water bodies.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best season to buy fish fingerlings?

The ideal time depends on the fish species and your region's climate. Generally, spring (February-April) is best for most species as water temperatures become favorable for growth. We can advise you on the optimal timing based on your specific location and fish species.

What is your minimum order quantity?

Our minimum order is 1,000 fingerlings for most species. However, we encourage first-time farmers to start with at least 5,000 fingerlings to achieve better economies of scale in their operations.

How do you transport live fish?

We use specialized oxygenated transport bags for small quantities and live fish transport tanks for larger orders. Our delivery vehicles are equipped to maintain optimal water conditions during transit to ensure high survival rates.

Do you provide technical support after purchase?

Yes, we offer free basic technical support for all our customers. Our experts can advise on pond preparation, feeding schedules, water quality management, and disease prevention. We want your fish farming venture to succeed!
